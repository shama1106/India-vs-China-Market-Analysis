## 📄 Executive Summary

This report provides the Australian Trade Commission with an analysis and comparison of the attractiveness of India and China to Australian companies seeking international expansion and international investment. Four dimensions are analyzed: the political environment, the economic environment, socio-cultural factors and the legal environment.

Research and analysis have shown that China comparatively exhibits higher political stability and more predictable regulation than India. However, India exhibits higher growth rates and more favorable ease of doing business indices. On the socio-cultural front, both countries face challenges and opportunities, with China's consumer base being more similar but lacking in diversity and purchasing power for certain goods. India, on the other hand, has greater cultural diversity across regions. Both countries have complex legal environments, with China having better IPR protection mechanisms.

Based on the analysis, both India and China offer some opportunities, but there are also greater risks. It is recommended that Austrade provide advisory services tailored to the specific needs of Australian businesses to minimize the risk of investing in both countries and avoid unnecessary complications.

## 💡 Key Findings

- India has **higher recent GDP growth** and better ease-of-doing-business scores
- China offers **stronger political stability** and IP protection mechanisms
- Both markets present opportunities in **e-commerce, healthcare and renewables**
- Recommended entry strategies: **Joint Ventures** (India), **Acquisitions** (China)
- Both countries carry **political, economic, social and technological risks** requiring careful management
