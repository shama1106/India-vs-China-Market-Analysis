Executive Summary
This report analyzes and compares the attractiveness of India and China as destinations for Australian companies seeking international expansion. Research shows China exhibits higher political stability and more predictable regulation, while India demonstrates stronger recent GDP growth and more favorable ease-of-doing-business indices. Both nations present significant opportunities in e-commerce, renewable energy, and healthcare — alongside complex legal, social, and technological risks requiring tailored advisory strategies from Austrade.

Risk Assessment
Political Risk

India: regulatory uncertainty from democratic policy shifts. China: centralized decision-making can trigger sudden regulatory crackdowns affecting foreign firms.

Economic Risk

Currency fluctuations, inflationary pressures, and global trade conflicts affect market demand and business confidence across both economies.

Social Risk

Cultural and language barriers, labor disputes, and demographic shifts — aging in China, rapid urbanization in India — impact supply chains and workforce planning.

Technological Risk

Rapid innovation disrupts legacy models. Cybersecurity threats and IPR vulnerabilities require ongoing investment in both markets to protect assets.

Entry Strategies
1
Joint Ventures
Leverage local partner knowledge, networks, and resources — especially effective in India for IT and renewable energy sectors.
2
Acquisitions
Buy into established customer bases and infrastructure — well-suited to China's fast-moving technology and healthcare markets.
3
Strategic Partnerships
Cost-effective, low-risk alliances ideal for market testing before committing significant capital in either country.
4
Wholly Owned Subsidiaries
Full control, higher investment burden — best reserved for firms with ample resources and established market confidence.
Key Recommendations
India Prioritize joint ventures or strategic partnerships in IT and renewable energy, leveraging India's growing middle class, rising digital adoption, and favorable ease-of-entry indices.
China Favour acquisitions and strategic partnerships in technology and healthcare, targeting the aging population's needs and the government's clean energy transition agenda.
Austrade Provide sector-specific advisory services with clear risk mitigation plans addressing regulatory, currency exchange, and socio-cultural barriers in both markets.
